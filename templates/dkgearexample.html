Nitty Gritty

Now to conclude this example, I have to point out a major factor of a simulation and why this data isn't 100% accurate. For simplicities sake, this data was all pulled from one simulation, which mean that in one of the examples you could of 'high-rolled' RNG (random number generation) and in another you could have very bad luck. To briefly explain that, inside a simulation, and also inside the game, an item might have a range of damage from say 1 to 100. And in one example luck could be on your side, and you only roll 99's and 100's; but in another example you might have no luck and roll a few 1's and 2's which would greatly affect you damage done.
